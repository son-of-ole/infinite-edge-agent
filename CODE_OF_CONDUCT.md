# Code of Conduct

## Standard

Project spaces should stay technical, direct, and respectful. Disagreement is acceptable; harassment, threats, personal attacks, and intentionally disruptive behavior are not.

## Expected Behavior

- Keep feedback focused on code, evidence, docs, and reproducible behavior.
- Be clear about benchmark limits and hardware-specific results.
- Respect privacy boundaries around user memory, hosted telemetry, and local device traces.
- Credit external projects, papers, models, and datasets when they shape the work.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Publishing private prompts, user memory, endpoint secrets, or non-public benchmark artifacts.
- Misrepresenting benchmark or production-readiness results.
- Harassment, discrimination, threats, or repeated hostile conduct.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues, block participants, or revoke contribution access when behavior materially harms project safety or technical integrity.
